Hypertension

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a chronic condition marked by persistently elevated arterial blood pressure. Managing hypertension involves lifestyle changes like a healthy diet, regular exercise, weight management, limiting alcohol, quitting smoking, and managing stress. Medications aim to lower blood pressure and reduce complications.

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels due to insufficient insulin production or impaired function. Type 1 diabetes results from immune system attack on insulin-producing cells, while type 2 involves insulin resistance or inadequate production. Complications can affect the eyes, kidneys, nerves, and cardiovascular system. Managing diabetes involves lifelong monitoring, healthy eating, physical activity, and medication or insulin therapy under medical guidance.

Heart Health

Heart disease, or cardiovascular disease, encompasses conditions affecting the heart's structure and blood vessels. Examples include coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and valvular heart disease. Risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, obesity, and inactivity. Prevention and management involve lifestyle changes like a heart-healthy diet, regular exercise, not smoking, and medical care under healthcare professionals.

Anemia

Anemia is characterized by a decrease in the number of red blood cells or hemoglobin within the red blood cells. Causes include nutritional deficiencies (such as iron, vitamin B12, or folate), chronic diseases, inherited disorders, or blood loss. Treatment varies based on the underlying cause and may involve dietary changes, supplements, medications, or other interventions as advised by healthcare professionals.
